## 1. PROSPECT PROFILE, TWO DIMENSIONS PER PROSPECT

SIZE TIER (Mark, 3 June 2026):
- Tier 1: 25,000+ devices/month. Large operators / retailers. Multi stakeholder, year plus cycle.
- Tier 2: 5,000-25,000 devices/month. Mid market. 3-6 month cycle.
- Tier 3: 2,000-5,000 devices/month. Smaller players. 1-3 month cycle, often single decision maker.

Country floor:
- UK: 1,000+/month
- Europe: 2,000+/month
- Below floor: case by case

INSURANCE STATE:
- Greenfield: no existing offer, OR ~1-2% SIM free attach
- Annual recurring: existing one off / annual offer with meaningful attach
- Monthly recurring: existing monthly recurring on SIM free

Combine both per prospect. Priority for outbound resource:
- Highest: T1 / T2 Greenfield
- High: T1 / T2 Annual recurring, T3 Greenfield
- Medium: T1 / T2 Monthly recurring with optimisation angle, T3 Annual recurring
- Lower: T3 Monthly recurring

Sweet spot signal (Paul, 21 May 2026): 1,000+ devices/month online (7,500+ if new country build), off the peg platform (Shopify green light), limited internal checkout control, no insurance or only one off annual with poor attachment. Four signals = near certain fit.

Deprioritise for 2026: pure affinity opportunities without hardware sale trigger, packaged bank account plays, BNPL only without device cover.

## 2. PIER PROTECT QUALIFYING TEST, RUN BEFORE DRAFTING

1. Hardware sale check (CCE): does prospect sell the hardware?
2. Geography check: UK or Europe? Both fit. Switzerland harder.
3. Checkout billing structural check (Monthly recurring state only): does their checkout already handle recurring monthly billing embedded? If yes, Pier's mechanic collapses.
4. Size + state classification.

## 3. DOOR IN FRAMINGS BY INSURANCE STATE

Greenfield state:
- Headline: additional revenue stream at zero implementation cost, fully managed.
- Frame: "tell us, we do everything else".
- No IT integration required from reseller. Line of copy on site + daily customer file to Pier. Pier runs everything else.
- Confirmed close: "Would you be interested in adding a recurring revenue stream to your business that you don't have today?"

Annual recurring state:
- Headline: recurring revenue model alongside what they already do.
- Three part argument: attach rate uplift (4-5x partner outcome, NOT specific percentages), quality of earnings shift, margin multiplier (~3x per device gross margin flowing 100% to bottom line).
- Confirmed opener (Mark verbatim): "Had a look at your journey. Similar clients we've worked with have had a similar journey, and we've been able to 4-5x their insurance attachment and revenue. Do you mind me asking what level of attachment rate you're getting from insurance?"
- Branches:
  - Low number (5-6%): "If we could double or treble that, would you be interested in having a conversation?"
  - High number (30%+): "You're doing really well. Maybe higher commission share or optimisation worth comparing notes on?"
  - Refuses: "Fair enough, would it be useful to model what a partner with your shape of business typically achieves?"

Monthly recurring state:
- Headline: pillar three, optimisation expertise.
- Frame: peer to peer curiosity, not pitch.
- Pre draft check: confirm checkout doesn't already solve embedded recurring billing.

Counter: "We've invested a year. A team trying to replicate this would take about three years to get there. And by then we'll be further on." Only when raised.

Risk aversion stall: "Try it. Two week go live. Not going to cost you a penny. Try it. If it doesn't work, pull it." Doesn't work for T1 with mature internal teams; frame as pilot with success criteria there.

4. Lloyd's of London formally recognises CLIP.
5. Alternative membership schemes are HIGHER risk than CLIP.

## 6d. IN SHOP / PHYSICAL RETAIL MECHANIC

For hybrid retailers (Easycash France 165 stores, Media Markt Saturn, FNAC).

Pier provides: product literature, pricing tables, staff training. No free month in physical retail.
